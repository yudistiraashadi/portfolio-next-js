
The AI Powered HR Management System is a comprehensive human resources platform that integrates face recognition, location-based verification, and automated payroll to modernize workforce administration. Designed for organizations that require secure, verifiable attendance tracking, the system replaces manual timekeeping with AI-driven biometric authentication.

## Key Features

### 1. Face Recognition Attendance

Employees clock in and out using facial biometrics captured through standard device cameras. The recognition engine achieves 99%+ accuracy by comparing live captures against enrolled face embeddings, eliminating the risk of buddy punching and proxy attendance.

### 2. Liveness Detection

An anti-spoofing layer ensures that only live, physically present individuals can register attendance. The liveness module detects and rejects static photos, video replays, and printed masks, maintaining the integrity of every recorded clock event.

### 3. Geo-Fencing and Location Verification

Each attendance event is validated against predefined geographic boundaries. Employees must be within the designated work area when they submit their clock-in, preventing remote or off-site check-ins from unauthorized locations.

### 4. Payroll Integration

Validated attendance data flows directly into payroll calculations. Working hours, overtime, and absences are automatically aggregated per pay period, reducing manual data entry and ensuring that compensation accurately reflects actual attendance.

### 5. Administrative Dashboard

HR managers access real-time attendance summaries, absence alerts, and headcount reports through a centralized dashboard. Drill-down views per department or individual make it straightforward to audit records and respond to anomalies quickly.

## Purpose and Benefits

By combining AI face recognition with geo-fencing and liveness detection, the system delivers tamper-resistant attendance data that organizations can trust. Automated payroll integration further reduces administrative overhead, allowing HR teams to focus on strategic workforce development rather than manual record-keeping.
