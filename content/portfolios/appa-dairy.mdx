
APPA Dairy Management System is a web-based platform for managing dairy farm operations within the APPA cooperative network. The system centralizes cow records, milk production tracking, financial management, and employee attendance into a single dashboard — replacing manual logbooks and fragmented spreadsheets.

## Key Features

### 1. Cow Management

Maintains detailed records for each cow in the herd, including identification, health history, and milking schedules. Farmers can track individual animals across their lifecycle from acquisition to retirement.

### 2. Milk Production Tracking

Records daily milking sessions and aggregates yield data per cow and per day. Historical production trends help farmers identify high-performing animals and detect early signs of health issues through production drops.

### 3. Milk Deposit Management

Tracks milk deliveries to collection points and cooperatives with quantity records and deposit history. Farmers have a clear audit trail of what was produced versus what was delivered.

### 4. Farm Cash Flow

Manages income and expenditure records at the farm level. Tracks revenue from milk sales against operational costs — feed, veterinary services, equipment — providing visibility into farm profitability.

### 5. Asset and Equipment Inventory

Catalogs farm assets including milking equipment, barn infrastructure, and vehicles. Helps cooperatives track equipment condition and plan maintenance or replacement cycles.

### 6. Visual Documentation

Integrated webcam capture allows farmers to photograph milking equipment conditions and barn environments directly within the application, creating a visual record for inspections and quality assurance.

## Purpose and Benefits

APPA Dairy digitizes the operational management of dairy farms that previously relied on paper records. By centralizing production data, finances, and documentation in one platform, the system gives farm managers and cooperative administrators data-driven visibility into their operations — supporting better decisions about herd management, resource allocation, and cooperative performance.
